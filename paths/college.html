📚 College

College is where you manage real money for the first time — often while taking on debt. Student loans, credit cards, your first apartment, and internships all hit at once. Get these years right and you graduate with options instead of just obligations.

Your Goal

Get through college with the least debt possible, the start of a credit history, and real-world money skills — so your degree opens doors instead of just bills.

1

Manage Student Loans Wisely

Borrow only what you need, understand the difference between federal and private loans, and know your interest rates. Every dollar you don't borrow is a dollar (plus interest) you won't repay later. Track your total balance so it never becomes an abstract number.

Student Debt Guide
2

Build Credit Responsibly

A student credit card, used carefully, builds the credit history you'll need for apartments, cars, and lower interest rates. The rule: only charge what you can pay in full each month. This is how you build credit without falling into debt.

Credit Cards Without Debt
3

Live Below Your Means

Apartment living, food, and social life are where budgets break. Learn to budget the money you actually have, split costs with roommates, and resist lifestyle creep funded by loans. The habits you build broke in college serve you for life.

Build Your Budget
4

Earn and Intern

Internships and part-time work do double duty: income now, and experience that leads to a higher salary later. A paid internship can be worth more than its paycheck if it lands you a better first job. Start building your earning power before you graduate.

Understand Total Compensation

📚 The College Money Playbook

The goal isn't to be broke and miserable — it's to graduate with skills and minimal debt instead of maximum regret.

Borrow the Minimum

Loans are easy to take and hard to repay. Live on less now so you owe less later. Refusing an extra few thousand in loans each year can mean graduating years ahead financially.

Pay Your Card in Full

A credit card is a credit-building tool only if you pay the full balance monthly. Carry a balance and the interest erases any rewards and starts a debt spiral. Never charge what you can't pay off.

Budget the Money You Have

Build a simple budget around real income — job, aid, family help — not borrowed money. Knowing where every dollar goes is the skill that separates the financially stable from the perpetually stressed.

Invest in Earning Power

Internships, skills, and networking raise your starting salary — which compounds over an entire career. A higher first salary is worth far more than its first-year value.

The College Rule: Graduate With Options

Every financial choice in college either adds to your future freedom or subtracts from it. Minimize debt, build credit, live lean, and grow your earning power. Do this and your degree becomes a launchpad — not a 20-year bill.
