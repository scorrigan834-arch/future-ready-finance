💰 Budgeting in College

College income is unpredictable. Your budget needs to handle that.

Budget by Semester, Not Month

Financial aid often arrives in lump sums. Divide the total by the months in the semester to find your real monthly amount — don't spend it all at once.

Separate Fixed from Variable

Rent, phone, subscriptions are fixed. Food, entertainment, and transportation are variable. Cut variable costs first when money is tight — never fixed ones.

Track Your Dining Plan

Meal plans are prepaid — but running out forces expensive alternatives. Track your usage weekly to avoid a $300 off-campus food month.

Build a $500 Emergency Fund First

Before investing or paying down loans aggressively, get $500 saved. This covers most college emergencies without going into credit card debt.

The College Budget Rule: Income Sources First

List every income source for the semester: aid disbursements, part-time job, family support, scholarships. Add them up, divide by months. That number — not what's in your account today — is your real monthly budget.
