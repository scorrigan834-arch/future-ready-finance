🎓 High School

The financial habits you build now compound for decades. High school is the perfect time to learn how money works — before the stakes get high. First jobs, college decisions, and your first taste of financial independence all start here.

Your Goal

Build a foundation of good money habits and basic knowledge — so you enter adulthood ahead instead of learning the hard way.

1

Earn Your First Money

A first job — whether it's babysitting, a summer gig, or part-time retail — teaches you the value of money in a way no class can. Track what you earn, understand your first paycheck (including why taxes come out), and start seeing money as something you can control.

Understand Your First Paycheck
2

Open a Bank Account

A checking and savings account is your first real financial tool. Learn the difference between them, why a savings account matters, and how to avoid fees. Many banks have free student accounts. This is also where you start practicing the habit of saving a portion of everything you earn.

Banking Basics
3

Make Smart College Decisions

College is one of the biggest financial decisions of your life — and it happens before you even start earning. Understand what student loans really cost, how to compare schools by value (not just prestige), and why scholarships and in-state options can save you years of debt.

Calculate College ROI
4

Build Habits That Last

The teen who saves a little and understands compound interest beats the adult who earns more but spends it all. Learn how money grows over time, why starting early is your single biggest advantage, and how small habits now become wealth later.

🎓 The High School Money Playbook

You don't need much money to start building wealth — you need time, and you have more of it than anyone.

Save a Slice of Everything

Make it automatic: every time you earn money, set aside a portion before you spend any. Even 20% of a small paycheck builds the habit that matters far more than the amount right now.

Learn Before You Borrow

Student loans feel abstract until you're paying them for 10+ years. Understand exactly what college will cost and what it'll return before signing anything. The best debt is the debt you avoid.

Understand Compound Interest

Money invested at 16 has decades to grow. Understanding this one concept — and acting on it early — is the closest thing to a financial superpower you'll ever have.

Avoid the Credit Trap Early

You'll be flooded with credit card offers soon. Learn how credit works now so you use it as a tool, not a trap. Used right, it builds your future. Used wrong, it costs you for years.

The High School Rule: Time Beats Money

You may not have much to invest yet, but you have something no wealthy 40-year-old can buy back: time. Start small, stay consistent, and let compounding do the heavy lifting. The habits you build at 16 matter more than the salary you'll earn at 26.
