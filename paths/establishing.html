💰 Budgeting When Life Gets More Expensive

Mortgages, kids, aging parents, career changes — your budget needs to grow up with you.

Budget as a Household, Not an Individual

If you share finances with a partner, budget together monthly. Misaligned spending is the #1 financial conflict in relationships — a shared budget removes the guesswork.

Add a "Future Fund" Line

Beyond emergency savings, budget monthly for upcoming large expenses: home repairs, new car, kids' activities, medical costs. 00/month now prevents a ,400 emergency later.

Review After Every Life Change

New job, new home, new baby, new debt — each one changes your numbers. Rewrite your budget within 30 days of any major life event.

Track Net Worth, Not Just Cash Flow

At this stage, budgeting is about building wealth. Track your net worth monthly so you can see whether your budget is actually working.

The Establishing Budget Rule: Percentage of Income

Housing ≤ 28% of gross income  |  All debt payments ≤ 36% of gross income  |  Retirement savings ≥ 15% of gross income  |  If any of these are off, that's where to start fixing your budget.
