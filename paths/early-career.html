🚀 Early Career

Your first full-time job changes everything: a real salary, benefits, and decisions that shape your financial future. This is the highest-leverage stage of your life — the choices you make about saving, investing, and debt now will compound for 40 years.

Your Goal

Turn your first real income into a financial foundation — emergency fund, retirement savings, and smart habits — while your money has the most time to grow.

1

Decode Your Benefits

Your salary is only part of your compensation. A 401(k) match is free money, health insurance choices affect your wallet, and benefits like HSAs offer rare tax advantages. Understanding your full package is often worth thousands a year you'd otherwise leave on the table.

Start Investing Now

This is your single biggest advantage: time. Money invested in your 20s has decades to compound. Capture your full 401(k) match first, then build from there. Even modest amounts now beat large amounts later — starting is what matters most.

Build Your Safety Net

An emergency fund — three to six months of expenses — turns a financial crisis into an inconvenience. Without it, one surprise sends you to credit cards and into debt. This is the foundation everything else is built on; fund it before you invest aggressively.

🚀 The Early Career Money Playbook

These are the highest-leverage years of your financial life. What you do now echoes for decades.

Capture the Full Match

If your employer matches 401(k) contributions, contribute at least enough to get all of it. It's an instant, guaranteed return you'll never find anywhere else. Skipping it is leaving a raise on the table.

Automate Your Investing

Set up automatic contributions to retirement and investment accounts so saving happens before you can spend. Money you never see is money you never miss — and it compounds quietly for decades.

Fund the Emergency Account

Three to six months of expenses in a high-yield savings account is your buffer against life. It's what keeps a job loss or car repair from becoming credit card debt that follows you for years.

Negotiate Every Raise

Your income is your biggest wealth-building tool right now. Negotiating salary and changing jobs strategically can grow it faster than any budget tweak. A bigger income makes every other goal easier.

The Early Career Rule: Pay Your Future First

You will never again have this much time for your money to grow. Before lifestyle upgrades, fund your emergency account and capture every retirement match available. The 25-year-old who invests consistently retires wealthier than the 35-year-old who earns far more. Start now.
