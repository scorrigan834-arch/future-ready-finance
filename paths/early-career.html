💰 Budgeting on Your First Real Salary

Your paycheck is bigger than ever — and so are the temptations. Budget before you spend.

Budget Your Net Pay, Not Gross

Taxes, health insurance, and 401(k) contributions come out before you see your paycheck. Budget based on what actually hits your bank account.

Automate Savings on Day One

Set up automatic transfers to savings and retirement the day your first paycheck arrives. You'll never miss money you never see.

Freeze Your Lifestyle for 90 Days

When you start a new job, keep your current lifestyle for 90 days before upgrading anything. Let your savings rate rise before your spending does.

Budget for Annual Expenses

Car registration, holiday gifts, annual subscriptions hit you once a year but need to be budgeted monthly. Divide annual costs by 12 and set that aside each month.

The Early Career Budget Rule: Pay Yourself First

Before any other spending decision, set these three amounts automatically: retirement (at least enough to get the employer match), emergency fund top-up (until you hit 3 months), and student loan payment. Everything else is what's left to live on.
