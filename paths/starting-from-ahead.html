💎 Starting From Ahead

For those who receive an inheritance, a windfall, or grow up with family wealth. Having more doesn't mean you automatically know what to do with it — and the stakes of getting it wrong are higher.

Your Goal

Turn an advantage into lasting security — without losing it to taxes, lifestyle creep, or bad decisions made too fast.

1

Pause Before You Act

A sudden windfall triggers urgency and pressure — from yourself and others. The single best move is to do nothing for a few months. Park the money somewhere safe, let the emotions settle, and avoid major purchases or giving until you have a plan.

Calculate Your Net Worth
2

Build Your Professional Team

Larger sums create complexity that DIY can't always handle well. A fee-only fiduciary financial advisor, a tax professional, and an estate attorney protect you from costly mistakes. Fee-only means they don't earn commissions for selling you products.

Understand Fiduciary Advice
3

Protect What You Have

More assets mean more to lose and more to plan for. Umbrella insurance, proper estate documents, and understanding the tax implications of inherited accounts all become essential. Inherited IRAs and taxable accounts have very different rules.

Estate Planning Guide
4

Make It Last Generations

Studies suggest most family wealth is gone by the third generation. Lasting wealth comes from staying invested, living below your means, understanding capital gains, and treating the money as a foundation rather than a prize to spend.

Capital Gains Guide

💎 The Windfall Playbook

Whether it's an inheritance, a settlement, equity payout, or gift — the principles are the same.

Set Aside the Tax First

Some windfalls are taxable, some aren't. Inherited cash usually isn't, but selling inherited stock or property can trigger capital gains. Find out what you owe before you spend, so you're never caught short at tax time.

Pay Off High-Interest Debt

Before investing or spending, clearing credit cards and high-rate loans is a guaranteed return equal to the interest rate. Few investments reliably beat eliminating 22% credit card debt.

Resist Lifestyle Inflation

The fastest way to lose a windfall is to upgrade everything at once — house, car, vacations. Each upgrade raises your permanent cost of living. Let your wealth grow before your spending does.

Fund Your Foundation

Max your emergency fund, retirement accounts, and HSA first. These give you security and tax advantages that spending never will. A windfall is a rare chance to fully fund your future at once.

The Starting-From-Ahead Rule: Steward, Don't Spend

Treat a windfall as a responsibility, not a reward. The goal is to convert a one-time event into lifelong security. Park it, plan it, protect it, and let it compound — your future self and any heirs will thank you.
